Why are so many animals endangered?

Human action, especially in the last century, is most likely to blame for the current extinction. Every year, between 100 to 10,000 species, ranging from big plants and animals to microscopic organisms, are thought to become extinct by scientists.

This is between 100 and 1,000 times more rapid than previous extinction rates. When humans overfish and overhunt, pollute the environment, destroy ecosystems, and introduce new species to places, species might go extinct. Video below

Why we need Protected Area?

15% of the world's land would be under protection by August 2020. Although this fell short of the goal, there were enough concrete pledges in place to help the globe cross the finish line just a little bit late. This is a remarkable accomplishment in many ways, and it may be the biggest and most quickly coordinated reform in land management history.

These "protected area" may be vast, tightly protected areas like US national parks, extensively travelled landscapes like UK national parks, or even tiny urban nature reserves. Protected areas, a mainstay of worldwide conservation for decades, can halt or limit many of the causes endangering biodiversity, such as habitat loss, hunting, and pollution.
